**SWUFlood: Segmentation of flood-affected regions in UAV imagery using an enhanced hybrid approach integrating shifted window transformers with U-net**.


Jabraeil Ranjbar, Hossein Ebrahimnezhad, Mohammad Hossein Sedaaghi
Computer Vision Res. Lab., Electrical and Computer Engineering Faculty, Tabriz (Sahand) University of Technology, Tabriz, Iran

**Abstract**

Flooding represents a catastrophic natural disaster, resulting in significant human, economic, and ecological losses. Accurate and effective demarcation of affected areas is essential for catastrophe response. Whereas flood segmentation using data-driven deep learning strategies is effective, scarcity of data generally hampers its timely field application. Current hybrid approaches integrating U-Net with shifted window transformers are promising solutions. In this research, high-resolution UAV imagery is used for fast and accurate flood segmentation in challenging terrain for catastrophe management. We propose a novel hybrid architecture approach, integrating U-Net and shifted window transformers (SWUFlood), that integrates. This innovative approach is shown through results to improve flood detection accuracy and efficiency significantly. The SWUFlood algorithm strengthens research in UAV image processing. Performance evaluations on common UAV flood segmentation datasets yield excellent results with best metrics. The approach features great generalization ability and impressive accuracy even upon limited training data.


**Highlights**

•A novel hybrid model enhances the accuracy of flood area segmentation.

•Combining U-Net with shifted window transformers improves feature and context learning.

•The findings help emergency teams assess flood damage and allocate resources.

•The model is applicable to agriculture, urban planning, and environment tasks.

•This study encourages future work on hybrid deep models in remote sensing.
